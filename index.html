Recent Discoveries

First Binary Star Found Near Our Galaxy's Supermassive Black Hole

In a landmark discovery, an international team has detected a binary star orbiting close to Sagittarius A*, providing new insights into how stars survive in extreme gravitational environments.[5]

Hubble Witnesses a Galactic Survivor Story

The Large Magellanic Cloud (LMC), a neighbor galaxy, has survived a crash through the Milky Way's gaseous halo, though it was stripped of most of its own halo in the process, a tale witnessed by the Hubble Space Telescope.[4]

Water Vapour Detected in Smallest Exoplanet's Atmosphere

Using the Hubble Space Telescope, astronomers detected water vapour in the atmosphere of GJ 9827d, an exoplanet only twice Earth's diameter, suggesting a new class of potential water-rich worlds.[4]
